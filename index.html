Wolfgang Pauli on the Neutrino

"I have done a terrible thing: I have postulated a particle that cannot be detected."
~Wolf. Pauli

The neutrino is a fundamental particle that plays a crucial role in the universe. It is produced in vast quantities in processes such as nuclear reactions and supernovae, and yet it interacts very weakly with matter, making it incredibly difficult to detect. The study of neutrinos provides insights into the fundamental laws of physics and the workings of the universe.
